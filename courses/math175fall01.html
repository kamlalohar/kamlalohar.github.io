Course Description: This is the second part of a three-semester course in calculus. The emphasis of this course is on techniques, but we shall also be looking for applications.

Course objectives: This course is a continuation of Math 170. So the main objective of the course is to understand the fundamental concepts of differential and integral calculus in order to be able to apply them. On a successful completion of this course the student should:

1. Understand what an inverse function is, how the exponential, logarithmic and hyperbolic functions are linked and how the trigonometric and inverse trigonometric functions are linked. Know their derivatives and the role of these functions in integration. Be able to solve growth and decay problems. Know L’Hospital Rule (L’Hopital Rule) and the situations in which it applies.

2. Be able to integrate suitable functions using the various techniques of integration. Know what an improper integral is, be able to decide if a given improper integral converges or diverges and be able, in simple cases, to evaluate an improper integral that converges. Know the simple applications of integration to Physics and Engineering.

3. Be familiar with the basic terminology connected with sequences and series. Know and be able to use the properties of convergent sequences and series. Be able to use a suitable test to decide if a series converges or diverges, be able to compute the radius of convergence of a power series and be able to produce Taylor and Maclaurin series expansions of certain functions.

4. Be familiar with enough analytic geometry to know how to establish the graph of the curve traced by a point that moves under some restraints. Know the source of and the equations of the various conic sections: Parabola, Ellipse, and Hyperbola.

5. Know the polar coordinates, their relationship with the Cartesian coordinates, Polar curves and computation of areas bounded by polar curves.
